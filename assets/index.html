American Traditional (Old School)

Bold black outlines, a limited high-contrast color palette (red, yellow, green, black), and classic sailor imagery like anchors, swallows, roses, daggers, and pin-ups.

Bold lines High contrast Flash sheets

Neo-Traditional

Evolves American Traditional with richer color palettes, more shading, ornate details, and influences from Art Nouveau and Art Deco. Often used for portraits, animals, and florals.

Decorative Rich color Ornamental linework

Realism

Focuses on lifelike detail, accurate shading, and depth to mimic photos or paintings. Can be done in full color or black & gray, often used for portraits and nature scenes.

Photo-real Portraits High detail

Black & Gray

Uses only black ink diluted to create smooth gradients of gray. Popular for portraits, religious imagery, and Chicano-inspired pieces.

Monochrome Soft shading Classic look

Japanese (Irezumi)

Large-scale body pieces with flowing compositions, mythological creatures, koi, tigers, samurai, masks, and florals like peonies and cherry blossoms. Heavy linework and rich color with strong use of negative space.

Full body suits Myth & folklore Storytelling

Tribal & Polynesian

Bold black patterns built from lines, curves, and symbolic motifs, often wrapping the body. Includes Polynesian, Maori, Samoan, and other indigenous styles.

Pattern heavy Cultural Blackwork

Watercolor

Mimics watercolor painting with soft gradients, splashes, drips, and minimal black outlines. Often layered over line art or silhouettes.

Painterly Soft edges Color splashes

New School

Cartoonish exaggeration, heavy outlines, and neon-bright colors. Influenced by graffiti, comics, and pop culture with wild, playful compositions.

Cartoon High saturation Pop culture

Geometric

Built from lines, shapes, mandalas, and sacred geometry. Often symmetrical and combined with animals or abstract forms.

Lines & shapes Mandalas Symmetry

Dotwork

Uses dots instead of solid shading to build gradients, patterns, and depth. Common in mandalas, geometric pieces, and surreal designs.

Stippling Gradients Patterns

Fine Line & Minimalist

Very thin lines, subtle shading, and simplified designs. Great for small symbols, delicate florals, and understated pieces.

Delicate Low contrast Subtle

Illustrative

Combines elements of drawing, etching, comics, and fine art. Can lean sketchy or graphic, often mixing blackwork with selective color.

Art-driven Mixed techniques Storybook

Chicano

Rooted in Chicano culture and black & gray realism. Features script, lowriders, religious imagery, portraits, clowns, and neighborhood symbolism.

Black & gray Lettering Cultural roots

Biomechanical

Imagines the body as a mix of flesh and machinery, with torn-skin effects revealing gears, pistons, and alien tech underneath.

3D illusion Sci-fi Organic + machine
